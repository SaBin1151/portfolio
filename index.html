Macroeconomic Indicator Analysis

Objective: To identify and analyze the key drivers of economic growth in several southern African nations by modeling correlations between various macroeconomic indicators from the World Bank API. Process: I used Python and the Pandas library to programmatically fetch, clean, and structure time-series data. I then performed statistical analysis and used Matplotlib to visualize long-term trends and relationships between GDP per capita, foreign direct investment, and inflation rates. Outcome: The analysis produced a clear visual and statistical narrative of economic trends, highlighting the outsized impact of specific policy-driven indicators on regional growth. The project demonstrated a repeatable workflow for macroeconomic analysis. Technologies Used: Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, World Bank API, JupyterLab.

A Machine Learning Approach to Smart Beta Factor Investing

Objective: To design and backtest a market-neutral, long-short equity strategy that uses a machine learning model to generate alpha by dynamically learning the non-linear relationships between quantitative factors and future stock performance. Process: The strategy involved engineering over 11 quantitative factors (Momentum, Value, Quality) for S&P 500 stocks. Principal Component Analysis (PCA) was used to distill these into 9 powerful 'meta-factors', reducing noise. A LightGBM model was then trained on a 36-month rolling window within a rigorous walk-forward backtesting framework to predict top and bottom quintile performers for the subsequent month. Outcome: The strategy successfully generated pure alpha with a stable, low-volatility return profile. The final out-of-sample backtest (Jan 2022 - June 2025) yielded an Annualized Return of 3.32%, a Sharpe Ratio of 1.16, and an exceptionally low Maximum Drawdown of -2.00%. Technologies Used: Python, Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn, LightGBM, yfinance.
